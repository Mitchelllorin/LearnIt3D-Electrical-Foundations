## ANSWER KEY

### Section A: Multiple Choice

1. **B — CSA-approved safety boots**  
   They are a standard apprentice-level PPE item on many Canadian job sites.

2. **C — To prevent unexpected energization during servicing**  
   LOTO is used to protect workers from shock, arc flash, moving equipment, and other hazards caused by equipment starting unexpectedly.

3. **B — Electrical pressure that pushes charge**  
   Voltage is the electrical potential difference that drives current through a circuit.

4. **B — `I = V / R`**  
   This is one of the standard forms of Ohm's Law.

5. **B — Current**  
   In a series circuit there is only one path, so the same current flows through every component.

6. **C — Equal to the source voltage**  
   In a parallel circuit, each branch is connected directly across the source.

7. **B — Watts**  
   Power describes the rate at which electrical energy is used or converted.

8. **C — Energy used over time**  
   A kilowatt-hour is a unit of energy, not power.

9. **C — Copper**  
   Copper is widely used because it conducts well and is dependable mechanically.

10. **C — Require attention to termination methods and oxidation issues**  
   Aluminum is used in practice, but proper connectors and installation methods are important.

11. **B — 12 AWG copper**  
   In this course, 12 AWG copper is the common residential 20 A branch-circuit conductor size.

12. **A — In parallel across the two test points**  
   Voltage is measured across a component or source.

13. **B — Without opening the circuit when clamped around a single conductor**  
   Clamp meters read magnetic field around one conductor to measure current.

14. **C — De-energized and isolated**  
   Continuity testing on a live circuit is unsafe and can damage the meter.

15. **B — White**  
   White is the usual grounded conductor colour in Canadian residential systems.

### Section B: Calculation Problems

16. **Current = 5 A**  
   Given: `V = 120 V`, `R = 24 Ω`  
   Formula: `I = V / R`  
   Calculation: `I = 120 / 24 = 5`  
   **Answer: 5 A**

17. **Power = 2880 W**  
   **a. Using `P = V × I`**  
   `P = 240 × 12 = 2880 W`

   **b. Using `P = I²R`**  
   `P = 12² × 20 = 144 × 20 = 2880 W`

   Both methods give the same result, which confirms the answer.  
   **Answer: 2880 W (2.88 kW)**

18. **Energy = 135 kWh; Cost = $18.90**  
   Heater power: `1.5 kW`  
   Time: `3 h/day × 30 days = 90 h`

   **a. Energy**  
   `E = P × t = 1.5 × 90 = 135 kWh`

   **b. Cost**  
   `Cost = 135 × 0.14 = 18.90`

   **Answer: 135 kWh and $18.90**

19. **Total series resistance = 50 Ω**  
   In series, resistances add directly:  
   `RT = 10 + 15 + 25 = 50 Ω`  
   **Answer: 50 Ω**

20. **Total parallel resistance = 2 Ω**  
   For parallel resistors:  
   `1 / RT = 1/12 + 1/6 + 1/4`

   Convert to a common denominator of 12:  
   `1 / RT = 1/12 + 2/12 + 3/12 = 6/12 = 1/2`

   Therefore:  
   `RT = 2 Ω`

   **Answer: 2 Ω**

21. **Voltage across `R2` = 80 V**  
   Total resistance:  
   `RT = 20 + 40 = 60 Ω`

   Circuit current:  
   `I = V / RT = 120 / 60 = 2 A`

   Voltage across `R2`:  
   `VR2 = I × R2 = 2 × 40 = 80 V`

   A voltage-divider check also works:  
   `VR2 = 120 × (40 / 60) = 80 V`

   **Answer: 80 V**

22. **Minimum conductor size = 10 AWG copper**  
   The load is `24 A`, so the conductor ampacity must be at least 24 A.

   From the table:
   - 12 AWG = 20 A → too small
   - 10 AWG = 30 A → acceptable

   Therefore, the smallest acceptable conductor in the table is **10 AWG copper**.

### Section C: Short Answer / Labeling

23. **Diagram Labeling**
   - **A:** Ungrounded conductor / line / hot feed to the switch
   - **B:** Switched hot / switched leg from the switch to the lamp
   - **C:** Grounded conductor / neutral return
   - **D:** Bonding conductor connected to the metal box

24. **LOTO sequence — sample correct order**
   1. Identify the equipment or circuit to be serviced and all energy sources.
   2. Notify affected persons.
   3. Shut down the equipment normally.
   4. Isolate the energy source by opening the disconnect or breaker.
   5. Apply lock(s) and tag(s).
   6. Release or block stored energy where applicable.
   7. Verify absence of voltage using the live-dead-live method.
   8. Perform the work.
   9. Remove tools, reinstall guards, clear personnel, and remove lockout/tagout when safe to re-energize.

25. **Wire Colour Identification**
   - **a. Black:** Usually an ungrounded conductor (hot)
   - **b. Red:** Usually another ungrounded conductor (second hot, traveller, or switched conductor depending on the circuit)
   - **c. White:** Usually the grounded conductor (neutral)
   - **d. Green or bare copper:** Bonding / equipment grounding conductor
