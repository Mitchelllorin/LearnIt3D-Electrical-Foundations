## Module 2 Quiz — Voltage, Current, Resistance (Ohm's Law)

**Instructions:** Answer all 10 questions. Show working for calculation questions and include units.

## Questions

1. **Multiple Choice**  
   What is the SI unit of resistance?
   - A. Volt (V)
   - B. Ampere (A)
   - C. Ohm (Ω)
   - D. Watt (W)

2. **Short Calculation**  
   A 120 V load has a resistance of 24 Ω. Calculate the current.

3. **Fill in the Blank**  
   Ohm's Law can be written as `V = __________`.

4. **Multiple Choice**  
   Two resistors, 6 Ω and 6 Ω, are connected in series. What is the total resistance?
   - A. 3 Ω
   - B. 6 Ω
   - C. 12 Ω
   - D. 36 Ω

5. **Short Calculation**  
   A 12 V source feeds two equal 1000 Ω resistors in series. What is the voltage across one resistor?

6. **True / False**  
   If voltage stays the same, increasing resistance will decrease current.

7. **Multiple Choice**  
   Two 12 Ω resistors in parallel have a total resistance of:
   - A. 24 Ω
   - B. 12 Ω
   - C. 6 Ω
   - D. 2 Ω

8. **Short Calculation**  
   A load on a 240 V circuit draws 20 A. Calculate its resistance.

9. **Fill in the Blank**  
   A voltage divider works best when the connected load does not significantly disturb the divider **__________**.

10. **Multiple Choice**  
    Which field condition best matches Ohm's Law reasoning?
    - A. A loose terminal can increase resistance and create heating
    - B. Lower resistance always means lower current at the same voltage
    - C. Neutral conductors never carry current
    - D. Voltage dividers are used only on 3-phase systems

## Answer Key

1. **C** — Resistance is measured in ohms.  
2. **5 A** — `I = V / R = 120 / 24`.  
3. **I × R**  
4. **C** — Series resistances add: `6 + 6 = 12 Ω`.  
5. **6 V** — Equal series resistors divide 12 V equally.  
6. **True**  
7. **C** — Equal parallel resistors halve: `12 / 2 = 6 Ω`.  
8. **12 Ω** — `R = V / I = 240 / 20`.  
9. **Current**  
10. **A** — Increased resistance at a bad connection can create heat.
