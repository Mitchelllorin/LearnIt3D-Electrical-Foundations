## Module 12 Quiz — Troubleshooting & Diagnostics

**Instructions:** Answer all 10 questions. Use a systematic troubleshooting approach and safe test practices.

## Questions

1. **Multiple Choice**  
   What is the first step in a proper troubleshooting sequence?
   - A. Replace the breaker
   - B. Identify and verify the symptom
   - C. Megger the entire building immediately
   - D. Remove all receptacles on the circuit

2. **Fault Scenario — Multiple Choice**  
   A receptacle reads **120 V hot-to-ground** and **0 V hot-to-neutral**. What is the most likely fault?
   - A. Open neutral
   - B. Short circuit
   - C. Closed switch
   - D. Perfectly normal operation

3. **True / False**  
   Continuity testing should be done only on a de-energized and isolated circuit.

4. **Multiple Choice**  
   A megohm test is primarily used to check:
   - A. Conductor colour code
   - B. Insulation resistance
   - C. Branch-circuit frequency
   - D. Receptacle polarity only

5. **Fault Scenario — Multiple Choice**  
   A breaker trips **instantly** every time a damaged tool cord is plugged in. Which fault type is most likely?
   - A. Open circuit
   - B. Short circuit
   - C. Proper motor inrush
   - D. Normal voltage drop

6. **Fill in the Blank**  
   The safe test-before-touch sequence is: known live source, target circuit, then **__________**.

7. **Fault Scenario — Multiple Choice**  
   A bathroom receptacle is dead, but the branch breaker is not tripped. What is a common next item to check?
   - A. The ceiling light only
   - B. An upstream GFCI device
   - C. The utility transformer primary fuse
   - D. The service mast weatherhead only

8. **Multiple Choice**  
   What test tool is especially useful for finding loose lugs or overloaded connections by spotting hot spots?
   - A. Thermal imaging camera
   - B. Fish tape
   - C. Pipe wrench
   - D. Non-contact tachometer only

9. **True / False**  
   Documentation after the repair is part of proper troubleshooting practice.

10. **Fault Scenario — Short Response**  
    Lights flicker when a vacuum cleaner starts on the same circuit. Name one likely cause.

## Answer Key

1. **B** — Always start by identifying and verifying the symptom.  
2. **A** — Hot-to-ground voltage with no hot-to-neutral voltage usually indicates an open neutral.  
3. **True**  
4. **B** — A megohm test checks insulation resistance.  
5. **B** — Instant tripping strongly suggests a short circuit.  
6. **Known live source**  
7. **B** — An upstream GFCI trip is a common cause in bathrooms.  
8. **A** — Thermal imaging helps locate overheated terminations and overloads.  
9. **True**  
10. **Possible answers:** loose connection, voltage drop, or a heavily loaded circuit.
