## Module 9 Quiz — Residential Wiring Basics

**Instructions:** Answer all 10 questions using common Canadian residential wiring conventions and CEC-based practices.

## Questions

1. **Multiple Choice**  
   In a 120/240 V split-phase residential service, the voltage from either hot leg to neutral is approximately:
   - A. 24 V
   - B. 120 V
   - C. 208 V
   - D. 347 V

2. **Fill in the Blank**  
   The voltage measured from hot A to hot B in a standard split-phase dwelling service is approximately **__________ V**.

3. **Multiple Choice**  
   A common conductor size for a 20 A kitchen small-appliance branch circuit is:
   - A. 14 AWG copper
   - B. 12 AWG copper
   - C. 10 AWG copper
   - D. 8 AWG copper

4. **True / False**  
   NMD90 is a common wiring method for concealed dry residential branch-circuit wiring.

5. **Multiple Choice**  
   On a standard 120 V receptacle, the neutral conductor terminates on the:
   - A. Brass screw
   - B. Black screw
   - C. Silver screw
   - D. Green screw

6. **CEC-Based Multiple Choice**  
   Which group lists locations where GFCI protection is commonly required in residential work?
   - A. Bedrooms, attics, and dining rooms only
   - B. Bathrooms, kitchen countertop receptacles, garages, and outdoor receptacles
   - C. Closets and hallways only
   - D. Only at the main panel

7. **Fill in the Blank**  
   A 3-way switching system uses two special switches connected by **__________** conductors.

8. **Multiple Choice**  
   AFCI protection is commonly associated with many dwelling-unit branch circuits supplying outlets in:
   - A. Habitable areas such as bedrooms and living spaces
   - B. Underground vaults only
   - C. Service mast connections only
   - D. Utility transformer primaries only

9. **True / False**  
   A two-pole breaker is used to supply a typical 240 V residential load.

10. **Multiple Choice**  
    If a white conductor is used as an ungrounded conductor in a permitted switch loop arrangement, it should be:
    - A. Left unmarked
    - B. Re-identified as required
    - C. Used only as a bonding conductor
    - D. Connected directly to the panel cabinet

## Answer Key

1. **B** — Either hot leg to neutral is about 120 V.  
2. **240**  
3. **B** — 12 AWG copper is the common 20 A branch-circuit size in the module.  
4. **True**  
5. **C** — Neutral lands on the silver-coloured terminal.  
6. **B**  
7. **Traveller**  
8. **A** — AFCI protection is commonly used on many habitable-area branch circuits.  
9. **True**  
10. **B** — Re-identification is required where the code permits that use.
