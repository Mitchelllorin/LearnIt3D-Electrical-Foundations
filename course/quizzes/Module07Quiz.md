## Module 7 Quiz — AC vs DC Fundamentals

**Instructions:** Answer all 10 questions. Show full working for calculation questions and include units.

## Questions

1. **Multiple Choice**  
   A frequency of **60 Hz** means:
   - A. 60 volts per cycle
   - B. 60 cycles per second
   - C. 60 amps RMS
   - D. 60 milliseconds per cycle

2. **Short Calculation**  
   Calculate the peak voltage of a **120 Vrms** sine wave using `Vpeak = Vrms × √2`.

3. **Short Calculation**  
   Calculate the period of a **60 Hz** waveform using `T = 1 / f`.

4. **True / False**  
   In a DC circuit, polarity remains constant.

5. **Multiple Choice**  
   Why is AC widely used for power distribution?
   - A. It has no peak value
   - B. It can be transformed easily to different voltages
   - C. It never changes direction
   - D. It eliminates conductor losses completely

6. **Fill in the Blank**  
   In a three-phase system, the three voltages are separated by **__________ electrical degrees**.

7. **Short Calculation**  
   Calculate the peak voltage of a **240 Vrms** sine wave.

8. **Multiple Choice**  
   Which source is a common example of DC?
   - A. Utility transformer secondary in a house
   - B. 120/240 V split-phase service
   - C. Battery
   - D. 3-phase alternator output to a building

9. **True / False**  
   RMS voltage represents the effective heating value of AC compared with DC.

10. **Fill in the Blank**  
    In a standard Canadian split-phase residential service, hot-to-hot voltage is approximately **__________ V**.

## Answer Key

1. **B** — 60 Hz means 60 cycles each second.  
2. **169.7 V peak** — `120 × 1.414`.  
3. **0.01667 s** or **16.67 ms** — `1 / 60`.  
4. **True**  
5. **B** — AC is easy to step up and step down with transformers.  
6. **120**  
7. **339.4 V peak** — `240 × 1.414`.  
8. **C** — A battery is a DC source.  
9. **True**  
10. **240**
