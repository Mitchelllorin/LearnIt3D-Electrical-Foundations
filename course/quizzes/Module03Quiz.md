## Module 3 Quiz — Series & Parallel Circuits

**Instructions:** Answer all 10 questions. Show full working for calculation questions.

## Questions

1. **Multiple Choice**  
   In a series circuit, which quantity is the same through every component?
   - A. Voltage drop
   - B. Resistance
   - C. Current
   - D. Power

2. **Short Calculation**  
   A 24 V source feeds three series resistors: 2 Ω, 4 Ω, and 6 Ω. Calculate the total circuit current.

3. **True / False**  
   In a parallel circuit, every branch has the same voltage across it.

4. **Short Calculation**  
   A 120 V source feeds two parallel resistors, 30 Ω and 20 Ω. Calculate the **total current**.

5. **Fill in the Blank**  
   Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL) states that the sum of voltage rises and drops around a closed loop equals **__________**.

6. **Multiple Choice**  
   What happens if one component opens in a simple series circuit?
   - A. Current increases everywhere
   - B. Only one load turns off and the rest keep working
   - C. Current stops everywhere in that loop
   - D. Resistance becomes zero

7. **Short Calculation**  
   A 120 V circuit has a 10 Ω resistor in series with two 20 Ω resistors in parallel. Calculate the **total resistance**.

8. **True / False**  
   A short circuit is an unintended low-resistance path that can cause very high current.

9. **Multiple Choice**  
   Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL) states that at a node:
   - A. Current equals voltage
   - B. The sum of currents entering equals the sum leaving
   - C. Resistance is always zero
   - D. Parallel current is always equal in every branch

10. **Fill in the Blank**  
    Residential receptacles and lights are normally connected in **__________** so each load can receive full circuit voltage.

## Answer Key

1. **C** — Series circuits have the same current everywhere.  
2. **2 A** — `R_t = 2 + 4 + 6 = 12 Ω`, then `I = 24 / 12`.  
3. **True**  
4. **10 A** — Branch currents are `4 A` and `6 A`; total is `10 A`.  
5. **Zero**  
6. **C** — An open breaks the only current path.  
7. **20 Ω** — Two 20 Ω resistors in parallel equal 10 Ω, then `10 + 10 = 20 Ω`.  
8. **True**  
9. **B**  
10. **Parallel**
