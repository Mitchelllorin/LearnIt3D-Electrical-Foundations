## Module 5 Quiz — Conductors, Insulators & Materials

**Instructions:** Answer all 10 questions. Use the introductory ampacity values from the module where needed.

## Questions

1. **Multiple Choice**  
   Which material is the most common choice for branch-circuit conductors in buildings?
   - A. Glass
   - B. Copper
   - C. Rubber
   - D. Porcelain

2. **Fill in the Blank**  
   The conductor resistance relationship is `R = __________`.

3. **Ampacity Lookup — Multiple Choice**  
   According to the module's introductory table, the typical copper ampacity for **12 AWG** is:
   - A. 15 A
   - B. 20 A
   - C. 30 A
   - D. 40 A

4. **True / False**  
   As conductor temperature rises, resistance generally increases.

5. **Ampacity Lookup — Fill in the Blank**  
   A common introductory sizing match for a **30 A** copper branch circuit is **__________ AWG**.

6. **Multiple Choice**  
   Which cable type is commonly used for concealed dry-location residential wiring in Canada?
   - A. NMD90
   - B. Bare copper only
   - C. Service drop cable only
   - D. Coaxial cable

7. **Short Calculation**  
   A 120 V single-phase circuit draws 15 A and the total loop resistance is 0.40 Ω. Calculate the voltage drop.

8. **Fill in the Blank**  
   In common Canadian residential wiring, the neutral conductor is typically **__________**.

9. **Multiple Choice**  
   Which statement about aluminum conductors is correct?
   - A. They are never used in feeders or services
   - B. They require correct terminations and installation methods
   - C. They conduct better than silver
   - D. They are classified as insulators

10. **True / False**  
    Longer conductor runs can increase voltage drop even when the conductor is not damaged.

## Answer Key

1. **B** — Copper is the most common building conductor.  
2. **ρL / A**  
3. **B** — 12 AWG copper is typically shown as 20 A in the module table.  
4. **True**  
5. **10** — 10 AWG is the introductory 30 A copper value used in the module.  
6. **A** — NMD90 is common for concealed dry residential wiring.  
7. **6 V** — `V_d = I × R = 15 × 0.40`.  
8. **White**  
9. **B** — Aluminum is widely used, but proper terminations are essential.  
10. **True**
