## Module 10 Quiz — Breakers, Panels & Protection

**Instructions:** Answer all 10 questions. Show working for the load-calculation question.

## Questions

1. **Multiple Choice**  
   In a thermal-magnetic breaker, the **thermal** element mainly responds to:
   - A. Sustained overload current
   - B. Colour of the conductor
   - C. Ambient light level
   - D. Neutral voltage only

2. **Multiple Choice**  
   The **magnetic** trip element is designed to respond quickly to:
   - A. Slight under-voltage
   - B. Short-circuit current
   - C. Low power factor only
   - D. A loose cover screw

3. **Load Calculation — Short Calculation**  
   A continuous load is expected to draw **24 A**. Using the module method `breaker rating = load ÷ 0.8`, what breaker rating is required?

4. **True / False**  
   A breaker is selected to protect conductors and equipment, not just to match the connected load name.

5. **Fill in the Blank**  
   A device installed at a panel to protect against transient overvoltage spikes is called an **__________**.

6. **Multiple Choice**  
   In a downstream subpanel, neutral and bonding conductors are generally:
   - A. Always landed under the same terminal
   - B. Isolated from each other
   - C. Reversed for balance
   - D. Omitted if the load is small

7. **Multiple Choice**  
   A common protective match for a 14 AWG copper residential branch circuit is:
   - A. 10 A breaker
   - B. 15 A breaker
   - C. 20 A breaker
   - D. 30 A breaker

8. **Fill in the Blank**  
   A clear panel directory entry would be **"Kitchen counter __________"** rather than a vague label like "plugs".

9. **True / False**  
   Tandem breakers can be installed in any panelboard regardless of panel listing.

10. **Short Response**  
    Why must an electrician still treat the service lugs ahead of the main breaker as hazardous even when the main breaker is off?

## Answer Key

1. **A** — The thermal element reacts to overload heating over time.  
2. **B** — The magnetic element trips rapidly on high fault current.  
3. **30 A** — `24 ÷ 0.8 = 30`.  
4. **True**  
5. **SPD** (surge protective device)  
6. **B** — In subpanels, neutral and bonding are generally kept separate.  
7. **B** — 14 AWG copper is commonly protected at 15 A.  
8. **Receptacles**  
9. **False** — They are only used where the panel is listed for them.  
10. **Because those lugs can remain energized from the service even with the main breaker open**, so shock and arc hazards still exist.
