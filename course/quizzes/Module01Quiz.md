## Module 1 Quiz — Introduction to Electricity & Safety

**Instructions:** Answer all 10 questions. Use Canadian electrical safety conventions and standard trade terminology.

## Questions

1. **Multiple Choice**  
   In a metal conductor, actual **electron flow** moves from:
   - A. Positive to negative
   - B. Negative to positive
   - C. Ground to neutral
   - D. Bond to earth

2. **True / False**  
   A nominal 120 V branch circuit should be treated as harmless during service work if the load is small.

3. **Fill in the Blank**  
   The common Canadian residential conductor colour for the grounded conductor is **__________**.

4. **Multiple Choice**  
   After identifying all energy sources in a LOTO procedure, the next step is to:
   - A. Remove the panel cover
   - B. Notify affected persons
   - C. Install the new equipment
   - D. Measure resistance on the live circuit

5. **Multiple Choice**  
   What is the main purpose of bonding metal enclosures and boxes?
   - A. To increase conductor resistance
   - B. To make lights dim less
   - C. To provide a low-impedance fault path so protective devices can trip
   - D. To replace the neutral conductor

6. **Short Calculation**  
   A person contacts 120 V and the current path through the body is approximately 1000 Ω. Using Ohm's Law, calculate the current through the body in amperes.

7. **True / False**  
   Arc flash and arc blast mean exactly the same thing.

8. **Fill in the Blank**  
   A safe absence-of-voltage check follows the sequence: test on a known live source, test the target circuit, then test again on a **__________** source.

9. **Multiple Choice**  
   Which PPE item is a basic apprentice-level requirement on many job sites?
   - A. CSA-approved footwear
   - B. Running shoes
   - C. Welding shield only
   - D. Cotton gloves only

10. **Multiple Choice**  
    Which hazard is most directly associated with an unintended low-resistance fault through air between energized conductors?
    - A. Arc flash
    - B. Voltage drop
    - C. Semiconductor action
    - D. Resonance

## Answer Key

1. **B** — Electron flow is from negative to positive.  
2. **False** — 120 V can seriously injure or kill under the wrong conditions.  
3. **White**  
4. **B** — Notify affected persons before shutdown and isolation.  
5. **C** — Bonding helps clear faults quickly by providing a low-impedance return path.  
6. **0.12 A** (120 mA) — `I = V / R = 120 / 1000`.  
7. **False** — Arc flash is the intense energy release; arc blast is the associated pressure wave and debris.  
8. **Known live**  
9. **A** — CSA-approved footwear is part of basic job-site PPE.  
10. **A** — Arc flash is the hazard created by a fault through air.
