## Module 6 Quiz — Tools, Meters & Measurement

**Instructions:** Answer all 10 questions. Focus on safe procedure, correct meter setup, and proper test interpretation.

## Questions

1. **Multiple Choice**  
   On a digital multimeter, the black test lead is normally placed in the:
   - A. A jack
   - B. COM jack
   - C. V/Ω jack only when measuring current
   - D. Frequency terminal on the circuit

2. **Procedure-Based Multiple Choice**  
   Which dial setting is correct for checking a standard 120 V branch-circuit receptacle?
   - A. Ω
   - B. Continuity
   - C. VAC
   - D. ADC

3. **True / False**  
   Resistance should be measured on an energized circuit so the reading reflects actual operating conditions.

4. **Multiple Choice**  
   Why must a clamp meter be placed around only **one** conductor?
   - A. To increase voltage
   - B. So the magnetic field is not cancelled by the return conductor
   - C. To measure resistance directly
   - D. To avoid reading frequency

5. **Fill in the Blank**  
   When measuring current with a DMM, the red lead must be moved to the **__________** jack.

6. **Procedure-Based Fill in the Blank**  
   To prove absence of voltage safely, test on a known live source, test the target circuit, then test again on a **__________** source.

7. **Procedure-Based Multiple Choice**  
   Before using continuity mode on a switch or conductor, you must first:
   - A. Energize the circuit fully
   - B. De-energize and isolate the circuit
   - C. Move the black lead to the A jack
   - D. Clamp the meter around the cable

8. **Procedure-Based True / False**  
   After measuring current with a DMM, the red lead should be returned to the V/Ω jack to reduce the chance of a dangerous mistake on the next test.

9. **Multiple Choice**  
   A meter reading of **119.8 VAC** on a nominal 120 V circuit is usually:
   - A. A normal reading
   - B. Proof of a short circuit
   - C. Too low to operate any load
   - D. A sign the meter is on ohms

10. **Short Response**  
    In resistance mode, a meter displays **OL** across an isolated switch that should be closed. What does this most likely indicate?

## Answer Key

1. **B** — The black lead normally goes in COM.  
2. **C** — Use VAC for a 120 V AC receptacle.  
3. **False** — Resistance testing must be done on a de-energized, isolated circuit.  
4. **B** — The outgoing and return magnetic fields cancel if both are inside the clamp.  
5. **A or mA**  
6. **Known live**  
7. **B** — Continuity testing is for de-energized circuits only.  
8. **True**  
9. **A** — 119.8 VAC is normal on a nominal 120 V system.  
10. **An open circuit / no continuity** — `OL` usually means the path is open or beyond range.
