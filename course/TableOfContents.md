## Core Modules

### Module 1 — Introduction to Electricity & Safety ...................................... **5**
- What electricity is and how it behaves
- Hazard recognition: shock, arc flash, burns, and blast pressure
- Safe work practices, PPE, lockout/tagout, and hazard boundaries
- Electrical codes, standards, and trade expectations

### Module 2 — Voltage, Current, Resistance (Ohm's Law) ............................... **21**
- Voltage as electrical pressure
- Current as electron flow
- Resistance and material opposition to current
- Ohm's Law relationships and basic calculations

### Module 3 — Series & Parallel Circuits ............................................ **37**
- Current and voltage behavior in series circuits
- Current division and voltage equality in parallel circuits
- Total resistance calculations
- Mixed-circuit reasoning and practical examples

### Module 4 — Electrical Power & Energy ............................................ **53**
- Power, work, and energy fundamentals
- Watt, kilowatt, and kilowatt-hour relationships
- Load calculations and efficiency concepts
- Energy consumption in residential and light commercial systems

### Module 5 — Conductors, Insulators & Materials .................................... **69**
- Conductive and insulating properties of common materials
- Copper, aluminum, insulation types, and temperature ratings
- Ampacity, voltage drop, and conductor selection basics
- Mechanical and environmental considerations in installations

### Module 6 — Tools, Meters & Measurement .......................................... **85**
- Hand tools and insulated tool selection
- Safe use of multimeters, clamp meters, and continuity testers
- Measuring voltage, current, resistance, and continuity
- Measurement error, verification steps, and field discipline

### Midterm Examination (Modules 1–6) ............................................... **103**
- Written assessment covering theory, safety, formulas, and measurement
- Scenario-based interpretation of circuits and safe work practices

### Module 7 — AC vs DC Fundamentals ............................................... **109**
- Direct current and alternating current compared
- Frequency, waveform shape, RMS values, and polarity
- Single-phase and three-phase introductory concepts
- Real-world applications of AC and DC systems

### Module 8 — Transformers & Induction ............................................ **125**
- Electromagnetic induction and mutual coupling
- Transformer construction and operation
- Step-up, step-down, isolation, and turns ratio calculations
- Practical implications for distribution and control circuits

### Module 9 — Residential Wiring Basics ........................................... **141**
- Canadian residential branch circuits and device boxes
- Receptacles, switches, lighting outlets, and cable methods
- Grounded, ungrounded, and bonding conductors
- Basic layout interpretation and installation planning

### Module 10 — Breakers, Panels & Protection ....................................... **159**
- Panelboards, busbars, and overcurrent devices
- Standard breaker ratings and common applications
- Ground-fault and arc-fault protection requirements
- Service equipment, disconnects, and coordination basics

### Module 11 — Motors, Loads & Applications ........................................ **177**
- Motor operating principles and rotating magnetic fields
- Locked-rotor current, horsepower, torque, and load behavior
- Common motor applications in building systems and industry
- Protective devices, starters, and practical field considerations

### Module 12 — Troubleshooting & Diagnostics ....................................... **195**
- Systematic fault isolation
- Reading symptoms, testing safely, and confirming failure points
- Open circuits, short circuits, overloads, and intermittent faults
- Documentation, corrective actions, and restart verification

### Final Examination (Modules 1–12) ............................................... **213**
- Comprehensive theory and applied problem-solving assessment
- Safety-first diagnostic and wiring interpretation questions
