Full Lesson Text
Residential wiring commonly includes service equipment, panelboard, branch circuits, outlet boxes, switches, receptacles, and luminaires. Branch circuits distribute power from breakers to loads.

Typical colour use: black/red as ungrounded conductors (hots), white as grounded conductor (neutral), and green/bare as bonding conductor. Conductors must be terminated correctly to avoid reverse polarity, open neutrals, and unsafe metal-enclosure conditions.

Switch loops, multi-wire circuits, and three-way switching require careful identification and labeling. Box fill, cable support, and device rating compatibility are essential for code-compliant installations.

Bonding ensures exposed metal parts are connected to fault-clearing paths. Grounding and bonding errors are common causes of nuisance tripping and shock risk. Accurate diagram reading and disciplined conductor management improve both safety and troubleshooting speed.
