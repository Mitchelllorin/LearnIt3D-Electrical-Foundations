Module 10 — Breakers, Panels & Protection
Learning Outcomes
Explain panelboard structure and function.
Differentiate overload and short-circuit protection.
Identify common breaker types (standard, GFCI, AFCI).
Describe breaker sizing fundamentals.
Relate protective coordination to safety.
Full Lesson Text
Panelboards distribute incoming power to branch circuits via busbars and overcurrent devices. Breakers protect wiring by opening circuits during abnormal current conditions.

An overload is excess current over time; a short circuit is a low-impedance fault causing very high current. Breakers respond using thermal and/or magnetic trip mechanisms. Proper breaker rating must match conductor ampacity and circuit design.

GFCI devices protect people by detecting imbalance to ground paths. AFCI devices help prevent fires by detecting arc signatures. Both are critical in modern residential protection strategies.

Safe panel work requires boundaries, PPE, clear labeling, and careful torqueing. Misidentified circuits and loose terminations are frequent field issues. Good documentation and neat panel layout improve maintenance and emergency response.
