Full Lesson Text
Electric motors convert electrical energy into mechanical rotation. In AC induction motors, stator fields induce rotor motion. Motor behavior depends on supply voltage, frequency, and mechanical load.

Starting current can be several times running current. Protection components include overload relays, fuses/breakers, contactors, and disconnects. Improper protection leads to winding damage or nuisance trips.

Motor nameplates provide essential data: voltage, current, phase, frequency, horsepower, and service factor. Matching controls and conductors to these values is critical. Under-voltage, phase issues, and mechanical binding can raise current and heat.

Motor troubleshooting combines electrical checks (voltage balance, insulation, current draw) with mechanical checks (bearings, alignment, coupling, driven load). Always isolate and verify de-energization before touching terminals.
