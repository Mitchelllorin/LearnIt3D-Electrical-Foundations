Module 12 — Troubleshooting & Diagnostics
Learning Outcomes
Follow a safe, systematic troubleshooting process.
Distinguish open, short, overload, and intermittent faults.
Use measurements to confirm fault location.
Document findings and corrective actions.
Verify safe return to service.
Full Lesson Text
Troubleshooting is structured problem-solving under safety control. Start by confirming symptoms, gathering history, and identifying affected equipment. Review drawings and expected operating values before testing.

Core fault types:

Open circuit: broken path, no current.
Short circuit: unintended low-resistance path.
Overload: normal path, too much current.
Intermittent fault: appears and disappears under conditions.
Test in logical order: source, protection, controls, load. Compare measured values to expected values, and isolate sections to narrow fault location. Avoid random part replacement; verify cause before correction.

After repair, inspect terminations, confirm clearances, reinstall guards, remove lockout per procedure, and run controlled restart checks. Final documentation should include symptom, root cause, corrective action, and verification results.
