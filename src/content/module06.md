Full Lesson Text
Electrical measurement is a core trade skill. Tools should be rated for the task and environment, including insulated screwdrivers, strippers, torque tools, and approved test instruments.

A multimeter measures voltage, resistance, and continuity. Voltage is measured in parallel across test points. Resistance and continuity are measured only on de-energized circuits. A clamp meter reads current by clamping around one conductor, avoiding circuit opening.

Before testing, inspect leads, meter body, category rating, and battery condition. Confirm meter operation on a known source, test target circuit, then recheck known source (live-dead-live). This confirms both circuit state and instrument function.

Common errors include wrong function selection, wrong jack connection, testing energized circuits in resistance mode, and interpreting unstable readings without checking contact quality. Repeating measurements methodically improves reliability.
