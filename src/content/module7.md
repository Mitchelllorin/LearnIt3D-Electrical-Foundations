Full Lesson Text
DC flows in one direction with constant polarity. Batteries and many electronics use DC internally. AC changes magnitude and direction periodically, typically as a sine wave in utility systems.

In Canada, standard utility frequency is 60 Hz, meaning 60 cycles per second. RMS (root mean square) values represent effective heating/equivalent DC performance; this is why line voltage references are RMS.

AC is efficient for transmission with transformers and distribution networks. DC is common in control circuits, battery systems, and electronics. Modern systems often convert between AC and DC repeatedly.

Understanding waveform shape, frequency, and phase supports correct troubleshooting in motors, drives, and power supplies. Misunderstanding RMS or polarity often leads to wrong measurements and unsafe assumptions.
