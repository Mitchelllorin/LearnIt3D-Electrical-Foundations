Module 1 — Introduction to Electricity & Safety
Learning Outcomes
Define electricity, voltage, current, and circuit.
Identify shock, arc flash, and fire hazards.
Explain lockout/tagout (LOTO) steps.
Recognize basic PPE requirements.
Describe grounding and bonding purpose.
Full Lesson Text
Electricity is the movement of charge through a closed path. In building systems, voltage provides the electrical pressure, current is the flow of charge, and resistance limits that flow. Apprentices must treat every conductor as energized until proven otherwise.

Electrical hazards include shock through the body, arc flash from fault energy, and fire from overheating or poor connections. Injury risk increases with moisture, damaged insulation, and poor work planning. Safe work begins before tools are used: identify sources, isolate equipment, and verify absence of voltage.

LOTO is a mandatory control process. Typical sequence: notify affected workers, shut down equipment, isolate energy source, apply lock and tag, release stored energy, test for zero voltage, complete work, then remove lockout under procedure. Verification with a known-live/known-dead/known-live check is essential.

PPE is the last layer, not the first. At minimum, many jobs require safety glasses, hard hat, and CSA-approved footwear. Higher-risk tasks may require insulating gloves and arc-rated clothing.

Grounding stabilizes a system to earth reference. Bonding connects non-current-carrying metal parts so fault current can trip protective devices quickly.
