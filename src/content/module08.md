Full Lesson Text
A transformer transfers electrical energy between windings through magnetic coupling. AC in the primary creates a changing magnetic field in the core, inducing voltage in the secondary.

Turns ratio determines ideal voltage ratio:
(V_s / V_p = N_s / N_p).
If secondary turns are fewer, voltage steps down; if more, voltage steps up. Current changes inversely in ideal conditions.

Transformers are used in utility distribution, control circuits, and isolation applications. Isolation transformers improve safety and noise reduction in certain systems. Real devices have losses due to core heating and winding resistance.

Safe transformer work includes de-energization, proper fuse/breaker protection, and verification of nameplate data before connection. Incorrect tap or polarity setup can damage equipment.
