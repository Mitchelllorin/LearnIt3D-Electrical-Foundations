Full Lesson Text
Conductors allow current flow; insulators resist it. Copper is widely used for branch circuits due to conductivity and durability. Aluminum is lighter and often used in feeders and services but requires correct terminations and oxide management.

Ampacity is the current a conductor can carry without exceeding temperature limits. Ampacity depends on conductor size, insulation type, ambient temperature, and installation conditions. Undersized or poorly terminated conductors increase heat and fire risk.

Insulation types are chosen by environment: dry vs wet location, mechanical stress, UV exposure, and temperature rating. Material choice also affects voltage drop, installation ease, and long-term maintenance.

Good workmanship includes proper stripping, torqueing terminations to spec, and protecting conductors from abrasion and moisture. Material knowledge is both a code and safety issue.
