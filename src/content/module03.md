Module 3 — Series & Parallel Circuits
Learning Outcomes

Predict voltage and current distribution.

Apply circuit rules to field diagnostics.
Full Lesson Text
In a series circuit, components share one current path. Current is the same through each load, voltage divides across components, and total resistance is the sum of individual resistances.

In a parallel circuit, each branch is connected across the source. Voltage is equal across branches, branch currents vary by branch resistance, and total resistance is less than the smallest branch resistance.

For series: (R_T = R_1 + R_2 + ...).
For parallel: (1/R_T = 1/R_1 + 1/R_2 + ...).

Mixed circuits are combinations of both. Solve by reducing step by step: simplify series groups, simplify parallel groups, then repeat until one equivalent resistance remains. Use that value to find total current and work backward to branch values.

Field application: dim lights in one branch may indicate branch-specific resistance issues, while total circuit overload can indicate excessive combined parallel load.
