Module 4 — Electrical Power & Energy

Full Lesson Text
Electrical power is the rate of energy transfer. Core formulas are:

(P = V \times I)
(P = I^2R)
(P = V^2/R)
Electrical energy is power over time:
(E = P \times t), commonly billed in kilowatt-hours (kWh).

Example: a 1.5 kW heater running 4 hours uses 6 kWh. Multiply by utility rate to estimate cost. This is essential for load planning, customer communication, and system sizing.

Real systems are not perfectly efficient. Motors, transformers, and electronics lose energy as heat. Good design and maintenance reduce losses by tightening terminations, selecting proper conductor sizes, balancing loads, and avoiding unnecessary runtime.

Apprentices should connect theory to operation: high current often means high heating risk, so protective devices and conductor ampacity must be matched to actual demand.
